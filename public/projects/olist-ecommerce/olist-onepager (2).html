Project Overview

The Olist dataset is a comprehensive public resource containing transactional data from a Brazilian online marketplace connecting small sellers to customers nationwide. The dataset spans September 2016 to October 2018 - just over two years of transactional data - with over 90,000 orders, 32,000 products, and 3,000 sellers.

Each table captures a different part of the retail lifecycle - from order creation, payments, processing, and logistics. Delivery delays, cancellations, and seller performance via the reviews table are all tracked.

MySQL was used to build a clean analytical model and solve real-world e-commerce challenges across revenue, customer behaviour, product performance, delivery efficiency, and seller analysis.

"A real-world dataset that returns real analytical value - 90,000+ orders, multiple relatable tables, and genuine business problems to solve."

EER Diagram - MySQL Reverse Engineering

Executive Summary & Key Metrics

The marketplace shows steady growth and strong logistics efficiency, with 97% of orders successfully delivered at an average of 12 days from approval. Customer satisfaction remains high with an average review score of 4.09, despite moderate delivery delays.

Credit cards dominate payments at 74%, and Sao Paulo leads all cities in both order volume and total revenue. Health & Beauty leads product categories in both sales volume and revenue. Future optimisation lies in improving performance in slower categories and supporting underperforming sellers.

Recommendations

→Focus marketing on high-value cities - Sao Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.
→Investigate long-delivery sellers for performance improvement opportunities.
→Explore repeat-buyer incentives to increase the 3% retention rate.
→Promote credit card payments which offer the fastest clearance for sellers.
