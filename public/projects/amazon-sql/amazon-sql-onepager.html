Project Overview

This project involved analysing a dataset of over 20,000 sales records from an Amazon-like e-commerce platform. The analysis involved extensive querying of customer behavior, product performance, and sales trends using PostgreSQL.

The project tackled a range of SQL challenges including revenue analysis, customer segmentation, and inventory management - all grounded in real business problems that an e-commerce operation would need to solve.

The project also focuses on data cleaning, handling null values, and solving real-world business problems using structured queries. An ERD diagram is included to visually represent the database schema and relationships between tables.

"An advanced SQL project solving 18 real-world e-commerce business problems across revenue, logistics, inventory, and customer behaviour."

03

Data Cleaning

Cleaning Approach

The dataset was cleaned prior to analysis, covering two primary concerns: duplicate records and missing values. Each was handled according to the nature of the affected field and its analytical importance.

Removing Duplicates

Duplicates in the customer and order tables were identified and removed to ensure accurate aggregation and reporting.

Handling Missing Values

Null values in critical fields were either filled with default values or handled using appropriate methods based on context.

Null Value Strategy

→ Customer addresses: Missing addresses were assigned default placeholder values.
→ Payment statuses: Orders with null payment statuses were categorised as "Pending."
→ Shipping information: Null return dates were left as is, as not all shipments are returned.

06

Solving Business Problems

18 structured SQL queries were written to address real operational questions across the business. Each query was designed around a specific challenge and includes a constraint that raises the analytical complexity. Full SQL scripts are available on GitHub.

Q01

Top Selling Products

Top 10 products by total sales value - including product name, total quantity sold, and total sales value.

Q02

Revenue by Category

Total revenue per product category with each category's percentage contribution to overall revenue.

Q03

Average Order Value

AOV computed per customer - filtered to customers with more than 5 orders.

Q04

Monthly Sales Trend

Monthly total sales over the past year using LAG() to display current and prior month side-by-side.

Q05

Customers with No Purchases

Registered customers who never placed an order - solved via two approaches: NOT IN subquery and LEFT JOIN.

Q06

Least-Selling Categories by State

The weakest-performing category in each US state - using RANK() partitioned by state.

Q07

Customer Lifetime Value

Total order value per customer across their lifetime, ranked using DENSE_RANK().

Q08

Inventory Stock Alerts

Products with fewer than 10 units in stock - including last restock date and warehouse ID.

Q09

Shipping Delays

Orders where shipping date exceeded 3 days after order date - with customer details and delivery provider.

Q10

Payment Success Rate

Percentage of successful payments across all orders with a breakdown by payment status.

Q11

Top Performing Sellers

Top 5 sellers by total sales value - showing both completed and cancelled orders with success rate percentage.

Q12

Product Profit Margin

Profit margin per product (price minus COGS), ranked highest to lowest using DENSE_RANK().

Q13

Most Returned Products

Top 10 products by return count - with return rate as a percentage of total units sold.

Q14

Inactive Sellers

Sellers with no sales in the last 6 months - showing last sale date and total historical sales.

Q15

Customer Segmentation

Classifying customers as Returning or New based on return behaviour - listing total orders and returns.

Q16

Top 5 Customers by State

Top 5 customers per US state by order count - using DENSE_RANK() partitioned by state.

Q17

Revenue by Shipping Provider

Total revenue and order volume per shipping provider - with average delivery time using COALESCE.

Q18

Revenue Decline by Product

Top 10 products with the highest revenue decrease ratio between 2022 and 2023 using year-on-year CTEs.
