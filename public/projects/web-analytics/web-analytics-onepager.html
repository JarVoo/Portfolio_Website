01

Introduction

SQL and Python were used to analyse data from an e-commerce store that sells kids toys called Maven Fuzzy Factory. As a new company, this project follows their performance over the first several months of operation, taking on the role of eCommerce Database Analyst.

Working directly with the CEO, Marketing Director, and Website Manager, the analysis spans three core pillars: traffic analysis and optimisation, website performance and conversion funnel analysis, and business pattern analysis.

Each pillar addresses real business questions from real stakeholders - with findings feeding directly into bid adjustments, page redesigns, and A/B testing decisions.

"Appointed as eCommerce Database Analyst, working directly with the CEO, Marketing Director, and Website Manager to grow and steer the business."

Tech Stack

02

Problem Statement

Working directly with the CEO, Marketing Director, and Website Manager to help grow the business and analyse performance along the way - making recommendations to steer strategy across marketing channels and the website itself.

Traffic & Channels

Which marketing channels are driving the most traffic and conversions? Which to invest in or scale back? What are conversion rates across campaigns?

Website Performance

03

Analysing Traffic Sources

One month after the database launch, the analysis begins by identifying where traffic is coming from, how it converts, and guiding management to adjust bids to optimise marketing spend. Full SQL scripts are available on GitHub.

April 2012 Month 1

Conversion Rate from Session to Order

The Marketing Director required CVR to exceed 4% to justify the current paid click bids on gsearch nonbrand. A query was run across all sessions up to 14 April 2012.

The CVR came back at 2.9% - below the 4% threshold. Bids were reduced. Over-spending was confirmed.
10 May 2012 Bid Change

Weekly Session Volume After Bid Reduction

The Marketing Director bid down on gsearch nonbrand on 15 April 2012. Weekly session volume was pulled to observe whether the bid change had caused volume to drop.

Weekly Sessions - gsearch nonbrand
gsearch nonbrand is sensitive to bid changes - volume decreased after the bid reduction. To maximise volume without increasing spend, campaigns needed to become more efficient.
11 May 2012 Device Split

Conversion Rate by Device Type

The Marketing Director flagged a poor mobile experience. CVR was pulled from session to order, split by device type, to determine whether separate bids were warranted.

Mobile and desktop should not carry the same bids. Desktop significantly outperformed mobile in CVR - bidding higher on desktop was the recommended path forward.
9 June 2012 Impact Review

Weekly Volume by Device - Post Bid Adjustment

A new bid on gsearch nonbrand was placed on 19 May 2012. Weekly session trends were pulled by device type to measure the impact on volume across both desktop and mobile.

Desktop vs Mobile - Weekly Sessions
Desktop session volume increased after the May bid, while mobile volume decreased in line with its reduced bid. The bid strategy was working exactly as intended.
04

Analysing Website Performance

Understanding where customers land, how they navigate the site, where they drop off, and how A/B tests on key pages improved conversion rates and revenue.

9 June 2012 Page Views

Most Viewed Website Pages by Session Volume

The Website Manager requested an overview of the most viewed pages, ranked by session volume up to 9 June 2012.

Pageview Sessions by URL
The /home, /products, and /the-original-mr-fuzzy pages were the most visited. The logical next question: where are users entering the site?
14 June 2012 Bounce Analysis

Home Page Bounce Rate Analysis

Bounce rates on the home page were analysed to understand how many paid search visitors were leaving without exploring further.

Bounce rate of 59% - unusually high for paid search traffic which should be high quality. A new landing page experiment was proposed to test whether a custom page could improve this.
28 July 2012 A/B Test

Landing Page A/B Test - /home vs /lander-1

The Website Manager ran a custom /lander-1 page in a 50/50 test against the original /home page. Bounce rates were pulled for both groups across gsearch nonbrand paid sessions.

Bounce Rate - /home vs /lander-1
/lander-1 outperformed /home with a lower bounce rate. Management directed all paid nonbrand traffic to the new page.
31 Aug 2012 Trend Review

Bounce Rate Trend Since Lander-1 Rollout

The Website Manager requested a weekly view of the overall paid search bounce rate since 1 June 2012, alongside session volumes for /home and /lander-1, to confirm the new page was delivering sustained improvement.

Weekly Bounce Rate - Paid Search Nonbrand
Traffic switched to /lander-1 at end of July. Bounce rate fell from just over 60% to the low 50% range, with /lander-1 session volume exceeding /home as the primary entry point.
5 Sep 2012 Funnel Analysis

Full Conversion Funnel Analysis

A full conversion funnel was built tracking how many customers made it through each stage from the new lander page through to order completion, across six page steps.

Click-Through Rate by Funnel Stage
The /lander-1 page, /the-original-mr-fuzzy, and /billing page showed the lowest click-through rates - these became the priority pages for optimisation.
10 Nov 2012 Revenue Lift

Billing Page A/B Test - Revenue Lift Analysis

The billing page A/B test ran between 10 September and 10 November 2012. Revenue per billing page session was calculated for both /billing and /billing-2 to quantify the lift from the new version.

Revenue per Billing Page Session
$22.83 revenue per session on the old /billing page vs $31.34 on /billing-2 - a lift of $8.51 per billing page view. A major improvement.
27 Nov 2012 Revenue Estimate

Estimated Revenue from Lander-1 Test

The last objective was to estimate the revenue earned from the gsearch lander test - measuring the uplift in conversion rate from 19 June to 28 July 2012 for nonbrand sessions, then extrapolating across the months since /lander-1 fully took over.

CVR - /home vs /lander-1
CVR of 3.19% for /home vs 4.06% for /lander-1 - an uplift of 0.0087 additional orders per session. Across 22,972 sessions since the test, that translates to roughly 200 incremental orders over 4 months - approximately 50 extra orders per month.
05

Conclusion

In helping the team at Maven Fuzzy Factory, the analysis successfully guided traffic optimisation, landing page strategy, and billing page improvements across a full year of growth - each finding feeding directly into actionable decisions.

The project demonstrates how structured SQL querying and systematic A/B testing can drive measurable revenue impact even in the earliest stages of an e-commerce business.

Over $10,000 in additional revenue from the /billing-2 test, and 200 extra orders over 4 months from the /lander-1 improvement - both quantified through structured query analysis.
→ Traffic routing: Before the A/B test all traffic hit /home. After /lander-1 launched, all traffic was successfully routed to the new page with a 6% improvement in bounce rate.
→ Session volume and CVR: After bid adjustments and device-level targeting, session volume increased and the order-to-session conversion rate improved.
→ Desktop bidding: Bidding higher on desktop resulted in increased session volume and improved CVR for the highest-converting device type.
→ Billing page lift: Over $10,000 in additional revenue attributed to the /billing-2 test across the two-month test window.
→ Lander incremental orders: Approximately 200 extra orders over the 4 months since /lander-1 fully took over - around 50 extra orders per month.
